Drift-less Local Volatility PDE

Posted on December 17, 2017

Local Volatility, Drift-less, PDE, Numerical Method


In the last post, we have already drived the Dupire Local Volatility PDE. However, doing duffusion directly using the Dupire PDE is not a good choice, because its drift cause instability during duffusion. In this post, we are going to derive the drift-less PDE from Dupire PDE, for our future duffusion usage. Initially, we have \(C(K,T)\) as the call option price function as of \(K\) and \(T\). Then we define the normalized strike \(k=\ln(\frac{K}{F})\), or \(K=F\cdot e^k\). And we will have $$\frac{\partial K}{\partial k}=K$$ For the change of variable you need to write first: $$\tilde{C}(k,T)=C(K,T).$$ Taking the first and second derivatives of \(\tilde{C}\) with respect to \(k\), $$\frac{\partial \tilde{C}(k,T)}{\partial k}=\frac{\partial C}{\partial K}\frac{\partial K}{\partial k}=K\frac{\partial C}{\partial K},$$ $$\begin{split} \frac{\partial \tilde{C}(k,T)}{\partial k^2}&=\frac{\partial}{\partial k}(K)\cdot\frac{\partial C}{\partial K}+K\cdot\frac{\partial}{\partial k}(\frac{\partial C}{\partial K}) \\ &=K\cdot\frac{\partial C}{\partial K}+K^2\cdot\frac{\partial^2 C}{\partial K^2}. \end{split}$$ Taking the first derivatives of \(\tilde{C}\) with respect to \(T\),, and because \(K=Fe^k\), $$\frac{\partial \tilde{C}(k,T)}{\partial T}=\frac{\partial C}{\partial K}\frac{\partial K}{\partial T}+\frac{\partial C}{\partial T}$$ According to Dupire local vol PDE equation, $\frac{\partial C}{\partial T}$ is defined as following $$\frac{\partial C}{\partial T}(K,T)=-r_fC(K,T)-(r_d(T)-r_f(T))K\frac{\partial C}{\partial K}(K,T)+\frac{\sigma^2(K,T)}{2}K^2\frac{\partial^2C}{\partial K^2}(K,T).$$ Substitute \(\frac{\partial C}{\partial T}\) back to the original \(\frac{\partial \tilde{C}}{\partial T}\) equation, we get $$\frac{\partial \tilde{C}}{\partial T}(k,T)=-r_fC(K,T)+\frac{\sigma^2(K,T)}{2}K^2\frac{\partial^2C}{\partial K^2}(K,T)$$
